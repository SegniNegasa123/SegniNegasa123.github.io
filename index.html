About Me

I grew up in Nekemt, Ethiopia, in a country where 120 million people still navigate government, healthcare, and agriculture through paper-based systems. That gap is not a statistic to me — it is the environment I came from, and the problem I have chosen to spend my career solving.

I graduated from Oromia Development Association Special Boarding School — one of Ethiopia's most selective institutions, admitting only 4.3% of applicants — with an ESSLCE score of 519/600. As a student, I published two internationally indexed research papers, built software platforms adopted by institutions, co-founded a tech community, and graduated 45 students from a coding programme I built from scratch in an underserved town with no prior access to technology education.

My work sits at the intersection of applied AI, civic technology, and community development. I don't just build for scale — I build for the people who have never had access to the systems everyone else takes for granted.

03.

Research & Publications

Traffic Flow Modeling in Meskel Square Road, Addis Ababa, Ethiopia
Four-day observational study at one of Addis Ababa's most congested intersections. Applied calculus-based speed, density, and flow modeling in Python. Identified a strong inverse speed–density relationship (r = −0.72). Designed and deployed a live AI-integrated camera system for real-time vehicle and pedestrian tracking — pushing the project from academic analysis into deployable civic technology.
IEEE Recognized · OpenAIRE Indexed · r = −0.72 · 7.62 veh/m peak density
Python Matplotlib AI Camera System Calculus Models Urban Data Science
Biomimetic Cooling System: Nature-Inspired Architectural Thermal Regulation
Designed a passive building cooling system inspired by termite-mound ventilation — a centuries-old architecture used by the Dorze and Konso peoples of Southern Ethiopia. Applied Stefan's Law, Fourier's conduction equation, and the thermodynamic stack effect. Python/Streamlit simulation reduced modelled heat entry rates from over 50% to under 10%, demonstrating a zero-HVAC design viable for tropical climates.
OpenAIRE Indexed · Heat entry: 50% → 10% · Zero mechanical HVAC
Python Streamlit Fluid Dynamics Stefan's Law Fourier Conduction
